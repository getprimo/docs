---
title: "Disk encryption and recovery key escrow"
description: "Disk encryption is crucial for safeguarding data against device loss or theft, with recovery keys automatically managed and stored by Primo during the encryption process, ensuring easy access if a user forgets their password."
---

Protect data on lost or stolen devices by enforcing full-disk encryption across your fleet. Primo automatically enables encryption during MDM enrollment and escrows recovery keys so IT admins can always regain access without relying on the employee's credentials.

<Note>
  Recovery keys are only stored in Primo if encryption is enabled in your MDM security policies > **Profiles** tab.
</Note>

## OS Support

| macOS | Windows | Linux | iOS / iPadOS | Android |
|-------|---------|-------|--------------|---------|
| ✅ (FileVault) | ✅ (BitLocker) | | | |

## Encryption flow

1. Enroll the device into the MDM
2. Wait 3 to 4 hours for the helper to install and manage the recovery key
3. Restart the device (required only for macOS)
4. Wait another 3 to 4 hours: the recovery key will be saved in Primo

Recovery keys are stored per device in the **Fleet > Devices** tab.

Encryption duration depends on the disk size and the device's performance.

## On macOS

- Encryption is managed by **FileVault**.
- FileVault is automatically enabled during MDM installation.
- Encryption becomes effective after the next restart.
- The recovery key is stored at that point.

## On Windows

- Encryption is managed by **BitLocker**.
- It is automatically enabled after MDM installation.
- No restart is required.
- The key is stored as soon as encryption is activated.

## On Linux

Primo does not support automatic encryption for Linux devices. Enabling full-disk encryption on Linux typically requires a full disk reset and OS reinstallation, which must be done manually before enrolling the device.
