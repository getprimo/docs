From **MDM > Software**, you can deploy apps to your managed macOS, Windows, and Linux devices without ever leaving Primo. Primo supports four types of apps:

- **Fleet-Maintained Apps (FMA)** — a curated catalog of popular apps maintained and updated by Fleet
- **Apple App Store (macOS)** — macOS apps purchased through Apple's Volume Purchase Program (VPP)
- **Custom apps** — your own `.pkg`, `.msi`, `.deb`, or `.rpm` packages

<Note>
For iOS/iPadOS and Android app deployment, see [Deploy apps (iOS & Android)](/mdm/policies/manage-apps-smartphone).
</Note>

---

## Deployment options

When adding any app, you can configure the following options:

### Install automatically
When enabled, the app is automatically pushed to all matching devices. If left unchecked, the app will only be installed when you manually trigger installation on a specific device.

### Make available via Self-Service
When enabled, the app appears in the **Self-Service** portal, accessible to employees via the Fleet agent. Employees can install it themselves on demand.

### Install during Zero Touch
When enabled, the app is installed as part of the Zero Touch Deployment flow. This ensures that core apps are present before the device is handed to the employee.

<Warning>
Enabling **Install during Zero Touch** may increase provisioning time. Reserve this for apps that are truly required before first use.
</Warning>

## App patching

For eligible app types, Primo provides two automatic update toggles:

| Toggle | What it does |
|--------|--------------|
| **Maintain source installer up-to-date** | Checks once daily for a newer version of the installer and replaces it automatically |
| **Update app on all devices** | Automatically updates the app on any device where the installed version is older than the current installer |

## Add a Fleet-Maintained App (FMA)

Fleet-Maintained Apps are a curated library of popular software (e.g. Chrome, Zoom, Slack) that Fleet keeps up to date.

1. Go to **MDM > Software** and click **Add app**.
2. Select **Fleet-Maintained App**.
3. Choose the app from the list.
4. Configure deployment options: **Install automatically**, **Self-Service**, and/or **Zero Touch**.
5. Enable patching toggles as needed.
6. Click **Add software**.

## Add an Apple App Store app (macOS VPP)

<Note>
**Prerequisite**: You must have an Apple Business Manager (ABM) account with VPP set up. See [Deploy VPP applications](/mdm/policies/deploy-vpp-applications) for setup instructions.
</Note>

1. Go to **MDM > Software** and click **Add app**.
2. Select **App Store (VPP)**.
3. Browse or search for the macOS app.
4. Configure deployment options: **Install automatically**, **Self-Service**, and/or **Zero Touch**.
5. Click **Add software**.

<Note>
The source installer is always sourced directly from the Apple App Store — the **Maintain source installer up-to-date** toggle is not applicable. Auto-update is not available for macOS VPP apps.
</Note>

## Add a custom app

Custom apps are packages you upload yourself (`.pkg` for macOS, `.msi` for Windows, `.deb`/`.rpm` for Linux).

1. Go to **MDM > Software** and click **Add app**.
2. Select **Custom app**.
3. Upload your package file.
4. Configure deployment options: **Install automatically**, **Self-Service**, and/or **Zero Touch**.
5. Click **Add software**.

<Warning>
**Automatic install support varies by file type.** The following types do not support the **Install automatically** option:
- `.exe` (Windows)
- `.sh` (Linux)
- `.ps1` (Windows PowerShell)

Use `.msi` for Windows and `.pkg` for macOS to enable automatic deployment.
</Warning>

<Note>
Auto-update for custom apps is **coming soon**. The **Maintain source installer up-to-date** toggle is not available for custom apps.
</Note>

## Remove an app

### Disable automatic installation

Go to **MDM > Software**, find the app, and uncheck **Install automatically**. This stops future automatic installs but does not remove the app from devices where it is already installed.

### Uninstall from a specific device

1. Go to **MDM > Devices** and open the device.
2. Open the **Software** tab.
3. Find the app and click **Uninstall**.
