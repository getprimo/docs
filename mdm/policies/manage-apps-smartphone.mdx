From **MDM > Software**, you can deploy apps to your managed iOS, iPadOS, and Android devices. Primo supports:

- **Apple App Store (iOS/iPadOS)** — apps purchased through Apple's Volume Purchase Program (VPP)
- **Google Play Store** — apps distributed through Android Enterprise (managed Google Play)

<Note>
For macOS, Windows, and Linux app deployment, see [Deploy apps (macOS, Windows, Linux)](/mdm/policies/manage-apps-laptop).
</Note>

---

## Deployment options

When adding any mobile app, you can configure the following options:

### Install automatically
When enabled, the app is automatically pushed to all matching devices. If left unchecked, the app will only be installed when you manually trigger installation on a specific device.

### Make available via Self-Service
When enabled, the app appears in the **Self-Service** portal, accessible to employees via the Fleet agent (or the managed work profile on Android). Employees can install it themselves on demand.

### Install during Zero Touch
When enabled, the app is installed as part of the Zero Touch Deployment flow, so it is present before the employee first uses the device.

<Warning>
Enabling **Install during Zero Touch** may increase provisioning time. Reserve this for apps that are truly required before first use.
</Warning>

## App patching

For iOS/iPadOS VPP apps, Primo provides the **Update app on all devices** toggle:

| Toggle | What it does |
|--------|--------------|
| **Update app on all devices** | Automatically updates the app on any device where the installed version is older than the current App Store version |

<Note>
For Google Play Store apps, updates are managed automatically by Google Play on enrolled devices.
</Note>

## Add an Apple App Store app (iOS / iPadOS VPP)

<Note>
**Prerequisite**: You must have an Apple Business Manager (ABM) account with VPP set up. See [Deploy VPP applications](/mdm/policies/deploy-vpp-applications) for setup instructions.
</Note>

In ABM:
1. Go to **Apps and Books** and add the app to your ABM account.
2. Ensure you have a sufficient number of licenses assigned to the appropriate site.

In Primo:
1. Go to **MDM > Software** and click **Add app**.
2. Select **App Store (VPP)** and choose **iOS / iPadOS**.
3. Browse or search for the app.
4. Configure deployment options: **Install automatically**, **Self-Service**, and/or **Zero Touch**.
5. Enable **Update app on all devices** if you want automatic updates.
6. Click **Add software**.

<Note>
The source installer is always from the Apple App Store — the **Maintain source installer up-to-date** toggle is not applicable.
</Note>

**[Migration note]**: If you are migrating from another MDM, you must remove the VPP link from your previous MDM after adding your apps. This may temporarily remove VPP-managed apps from devices; they will be automatically redeployed once the new link is active. Do not share the same VPP token between two MDMs.

## Add a Google Play Store app (Android)

<Note>
**Prerequisite**: Android Enterprise must be configured. Ensure your Android devices are enrolled via Android Enterprise (managed work profile or fully managed).
</Note>

1. Go to **MDM > Software** and click **Add app**.
2. Select **Google Play Store**.
3. Search for the app in the managed Google Play catalog.
4. Configure deployment options: **Install automatically**, **Self-Service**, and/or **Zero Touch**.
5. Click **Add software**.

<Note>
Only **free** apps are supported through managed Google Play. Paid apps are not available for enterprise distribution via this mechanism.

App updates are handled automatically by Google Play on enrolled devices.
</Note>

## Remove an app

### Disable automatic installation

Go to **MDM > Software**, find the app, and uncheck **Install automatically**. This stops future automatic installs but does not remove the app from devices where it is already installed.

### Uninstall from a specific device

1. Go to **MDM > Devices** and open the device.
2. Open the **Software** tab.
3. Find the app and click **Uninstall**.
