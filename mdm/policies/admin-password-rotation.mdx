Automatically rotate local administrator account passwords across your fleet and store the new credentials securely in Primo. This reduces the risk of credential exposure from shared or stale passwords without requiring manual intervention from your IT team.

## OS support

| macOS | Windows | Linux | iOS / iPadOS | Android |
|-------|---------|-------|--------------|---------|
| ✅ | ✅ | | | |

## How it works

When enabled, Primo generates a new random password for the designated local administrator account at the configured rotation interval. The new password is immediately stored in the device panel so it remains accessible to IT administrators at all times.

Password rotation is performed silently — no user interaction is required and end users are not notified.

## Configure the control

1. In Primo, go to **MDM > Controls > Add new control**.
2. Select your target OS tab and choose **Admin password rotation**.
3. Enable the control.
4. Set the **rotation interval** (e.g. every 30, 60, or 90 days).
5. Enter the **username** of the local administrator account to rotate.
6. Save and apply the control to the relevant device group.

## Retrieve a rotated password

After a rotation, the current password is available in the device panel:

1. Go to **Equipment > Devices** and open the target device.
2. Navigate to the **Users** tab.
3. Click the eye icon next to the administrator account to reveal the current password.

## Troubleshooting

**The password was not rotated on schedule**
- Confirm the device was online and enrolled in MDM at the time of the scheduled rotation.
- Check that the administrator account username matches exactly (case-sensitive on macOS).
- Verify the control is still active and assigned to the correct device group.

**The retrieved password does not work**
- A rotation may be in progress. Wait a few minutes and retrieve the password again.
- Ensure you are looking at the password for the correct device — each device has its own unique credential.
