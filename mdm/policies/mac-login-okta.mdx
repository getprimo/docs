---
title: "Mac login with Okta"
description: "Replace local macOS logins with Okta single sign-on so employees use their corporate Okta credentials to unlock their Mac."
---

Let employees sign in to their Mac using their Okta credentials instead of a separate local password. This centralizes identity management in Okta, simplifies the employee login experience, and gives IT full visibility and control over Mac access through your existing identity provider.

## OS support

| macOS | Windows | Linux | iOS / iPadOS | Android |
|-------|---------|-------|--------------|---------|
| ✅ | | | | |

## Prerequisites

- An active Okta organization (Okta Workforce Identity Cloud).
- Primo MDM deployed on the target Mac devices.
- macOS 13 (Ventura) or later.

## Connect your Okta organization

Before deploying the control, connect Primo to your Okta organization:

1. In Primo, go to **Settings > MDM > Integrations**.
2. Select **Okta** and follow the authorization flow.
3. Grant the required permissions to allow Primo to manage device-bound credentials.

<Tip>
If you already use Okta for SCIM or SAML within Primo, you may be able to reuse the existing connection. Check your current integration settings before creating a new one.
</Tip>

## Deploy the control

1. In Primo, go to **MDM > Controls > Add new control**.
2. Select the **macOS** tab and choose **Mac login with Okta**.
3. Enable the control.
4. Save and apply the control to the relevant device group.

Once deployed, macOS devices will use Platform SSO to bind the login screen to Okta. Employees authenticate with their Okta credentials at the login window.

## How it works

Primo uses macOS Platform Single Sign-On (Platform SSO), introduced in macOS 13, to integrate the login window with Okta. When a user logs in:

1. macOS contacts Okta to validate the credentials.
2. On success, the user's local session is created or unlocked.
3. The Okta token is stored securely in the macOS Keychain for subsequent SSO to browser and app sign-ins.

Offline login is supported — if the device cannot reach Okta, macOS falls back to the locally cached credentials.

<Note>
The first login after enabling this control requires an active internet connection to establish the Okta binding on the device.
</Note>

## Troubleshooting

**Users cannot log in after the control is deployed**
- Ensure the device has internet access to reach Okta during the first login.
- Verify the user's Okta account is active, not locked, and not subject to an MFA policy that blocks device login.
- Check that the Okta integration is correctly configured in **Settings > MDM > Integrations**.

**The Okta login prompt does not appear at the login window**
- Confirm the MDM profile was successfully delivered (check the device panel in Primo).
- On macOS, Platform SSO requires macOS 13 or later. Verify the device OS version.
- Re-enroll the device if the profile delivery shows an error.
