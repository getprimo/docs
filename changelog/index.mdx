<Update label="March 15, 2026" tags={["API", "MDM", "SaaS", "Procurement"]}>
  ## Public API, custom packages, and per-country procurement

  A lot shipped over the past few weeks. Here is a summary of the most impactful changes.

  ### Public API expansion

  The Primo API now covers significantly more of the platform. You can programmatically manage [software](/api/create-primo-api-key), MDM Controls, Device Groups, tickets, and SaaS applications. If you build automations or integrations on top of Primo, these new endpoints let you do much more without touching the dashboard.

  ### Custom software packages

  You can now upload and deploy your own software packages directly in Primo. This is in addition to the existing Fleet Maintained Apps and App Store (VPP) support. Upload a package, configure targeting, and roll it out to your fleet — all from the [Software section](/mdm/policies/manage-apps-laptop).

  ### Per-country catalog and procurement

  Procurement in Primo is now organized by country. You can manage product catalogs, suppliers, and shipping settings per country, making it easier to handle international purchasing from a single place. Head to [Shipping countries](/procurement/shipping-countries) to learn more.

  ### ThreatDown endpoint protection

  Primo now supports [ThreatDown](/mdm/endpoint-protection/threatdown) as an endpoint protection option alongside SentinelOne and CrowdStrike. Deploy ThreatDown agents to your fleet automatically through MDM.

  ### SentinelOne on Linux and ARM64

  SentinelOne deployment now supports Linux devices and ARM64 Windows machines. If you manage a mixed fleet, you can deploy endpoint protection more broadly without manual installs.

  ### Windows PIN policy

  A new MDM Control lets you enforce PIN policies on Windows devices, giving you more granular control over authentication requirements across your fleet.

  ### iPadOS as a distinct platform

  iPadOS devices are now recognized and managed as a separate platform from iOS, allowing for more precise targeting when applying Controls or deploying software.

  ### Asset ID field on devices

  You can now assign and track an asset ID (asset tag) on each device. This helps teams that use internal asset numbering systems keep everything aligned in Primo.

  ### Device code and password history

  Admins can now view code history and password history for device users, making it easier to troubleshoot access issues or audit credential changes over time.

  ### Contractor flag on employees

  You can now mark employees as contractors, making it simpler to distinguish between employee types and apply different workflows or policies.

  ### EDR visibility on devices

  Device records now display endpoint protection (EDR) status directly. You can quickly see which devices have active protection and which need attention, with [EDR columns](/mdm/endpoint-protection/sentinelone) visible in device lists.

  ### Financial tracking

  The financial tracking section has been improved and moved out of beta, with a cleaner interface for monitoring device and SaaS costs across your organization.

  ### Collapsible navigation

  The main navigation panel in the dashboard is now collapsible, giving you more screen space when working in tables or detail views.

  ### Bug fixes and improvements

  - Fixed Chrome extension force-install behavior on Windows to prevent unintended removals
  - Resolved iPadOS enrollment and platform validation issues
  - Fixed OS update status detection on Windows devices
  - Improved recovery key sync for MDM-managed devices
  - Fixed SaaS task grouping for provisioning and deprovisioning workflows
  - Resolved various onboarding automation date and scheduling issues
  - Improved error handling for software deployment across platforms
</Update>

<Update label="February 11, 2026" tags={["MDM", "Software"]}>
  ## Software Management & App Patching in Primo

  You can now manage software directly in Primo, without switching tools. This update brings two things: a clearer view of what's installed across your fleet, and a brand new way to keep apps up to date.

  ### App patching

  Primo now lets you enable **auto-patching per app**, so updates are handled automatically. From any software page, you can turn on patching to:

  - keep installers up to date
  - automatically roll out new versions
  - use install scripts when needed

  It makes it much easier to stay secure and reduce manual update work.

  ### Install apps from Primo

  You can also deploy new apps directly from Primo. We currently support **Fleet Maintained Apps** on **macOS and Windows**, with control over:

  - targeting (device groups, specific devices, or all devices)
  - self service availability
  - install scripts

  ### See what's installed, at a glance

  Finally, the Software section now shows what's installed and where, with versions, filtering, and custom views to track the apps that matter most.

  _Coming soon_: custom packages, and App Store apps for iOS, iPadOS, and Android.
</Update>

<Update label="December 18, 2025" tags={["MDM"]}>
  ## MDM Controls and Software Management

  We just made a major upgrade to how device management works in Primo.

  ### MDM policies are now Controls

  MDM policies are now called **Controls**, with a dedicated space in Primo. The goal is simple: make policies easier to find, easier to understand, and easier to manage at scale. We also simplified naming:

  - Profiles are now **Device Groups**
  - Installed Apps is now **Software**

  ### Target devices directly, without profile gymnastics

  The biggest change is targeting. Instead of relying on MDM Profiles, you can now assign Controls **directly to devices**.

  Create a Control based on the platform, configure it once, then choose where it applies:

  - a device group
  - specific devices
  - or your entire fleet

  This makes rollouts faster, clearer, and much easier to reason about.

  ### One place to see every policy deployed

  You now get a single view of all Controls applied across your fleet, so you can quickly answer: what is deployed, where, and to whom?

  ### Duplicate policies with confidence

  Duplicating a policy was possible before, but it is now much more obvious and frictionless. It is the fastest way to reuse a setup and adapt it for another group or exception case.

  ### Profiles become Device Groups

  We are also simplifying the structure:

  - MDM Profiles are moving to **Controls**
  - Profiles are becoming **Device Groups**

  Device Groups are now what they should have always been: a clean way to organize your fleet, not a requirement just to deploy policies.

  ### Installed Apps becomes Software

  Installed Apps is now **Software**, with a clearer experience and a dedicated section.

  This is the first step in bringing more of Fleet's software management capabilities into Primo, so you can manage software with the same level of clarity as devices and policies.
</Update>

<Update label="December 10, 2025" tags={["SaaS"]}>
  ## Tags for SaaS

  Make your SaaS directory easier to navigate and act on with tags!

  Tags are now available on all SaaS apps Primo discovers through SaaS Mapping and the Chrome extension. You can add and edit tags manually to group tools by team, owner, sensitivity, risk level, and more.

  If you want Primo to do this for you, you can enable AI auto-tagging in **Settings \> SaaS tab**. Primo will then review your existing and newly discovered apps and automatically apply the most relevant tags.

  Once your apps are tagged, you can filter by tag, create focused views for audits or renewals, and spot security risks much faster.
</Update>

<Update label="November 17, 2025" tags={["SaaS"]}>
  ## SaaS Mapping and Chrome Extension

  Get a clear view of all the SaaS apps and work identities used across your company.

  SaaS Mapping is now available. It shows every SaaS app accessed with a company email through Google Workspace. If you need more coverage, you can add the new Chrome extension. It maps all work identities used with a company email, even when users are not signing in with Google.

  SaaS Mapping is enabled by default if you connected Google Workspace. You can deploy the Chrome extension through an MDM profile using the Chrome MDM Policy.

  This feature is available for all organizations with an active SaaS Management subscription.

  Primo only focuses on work-related SaaS usage. No personal accounts, no browsing data, and no content are collected.
</Update>

<Update label="July 31, 2025" tags={["Feature"]}>
  ## AI Automations: Smooth, Effortless Management

  AI Automations go beyond simple task delegation by automating your routine processes in the background, with no intervention needed from you. While Uno reacts to specific requests, Automations handle ongoing tasks automatically, based on your defined rules.

  For instance, in addition to having Uno send a reminder to a user who's late on updates, you can set up an Automation to send reminders to all relevant users on a regular basis, without lifting a finger. Likewise, once Uno configures an MDM setting for you, let Automations take over and apply the new settings across all devices in your organization automatically.

  _Here's how you can start using them:_

  - **Regular Audits**: "Generate a monthly report of all non-compliant devices."
  - **Automated Alerts**: "Email me every time a device falls out of compliance with our MDM policies." / "Send reminder emails to employees who haven't registered their devices after 7 days."
  - **Device Tracking**: "Email me monthly with a list of devices that have exceeded 80% CPU usage over the last 7 days, or whose batteries have passed 10,000 cycles."
</Update>
